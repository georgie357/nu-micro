Helminths
--- Slide 8 ---
Nematoda & Platyhelminths
Nematoda (roundworms)
>15,000
Unsegmented bodies, full digestive system
Common intestinal parasites
Egg is infective:
Ascaris lumbricoides: small intestines of humans
Trichuris trichiura (whipworm)
Enterobius vermicularis (pinworm)
Larvae are infective:
Hookworms
Trichinella spiralis
Heartworm (mainly dogs and cats)
Platyhelminths (flatworms)
Trematodes (flukes)
flat, leaf shaped worms; suckers hold organism in place and suck  fluids from host; complicated life cycle: Taenia
Cestodes (tapeworms):
have segmented, ribbon shaped bodies; head (scolex) may have suckers and hooks for attachment; no digestive system, absorbs nutrients directly: Schistosoma
--- Slide 9 ---
Fungi
--- Slide 10 ---
Types of Fungi
Molds and fleshy fungi:
Hyphae (filaments) form a thallus (body)
Hyphae can contain septa or not
Hyphae can be vegetative or reproductive (aereal)
Can form a mycelium (visible)
Yeasts:
Unicellular
Often divide by budding or fission
Can do aerobic or anaerobic growth (fermentation)
Dimorphic fungi:
Can grow as mold or as yeast, depending on temp
