--- Slide 2 ---
General characteristics of viruses
Acellular, small: 20-900 nm
Also called virions (infectious particles)
Obligatory intracellular parasites, use the metabolic machinery (enzymes) of their host
All contain DNA or RNA & a protein coat
Some are enclosed by an envelope
Most viruses infect only specific types of cells
Host range is determined by specific host attachment sites and cellular factors
Bacteriophages infect bacteria

--- Slide 9 ---
Viral nucleic acids are processed in different ways
DNA: Both can be transcribed by cellular or viral enzymes
Ds (double stranded) DNA (business as usual)
Ss (single stranded) DNA (strand needs to be copied)
RNA
Ss +RNA: used as mRNA to make RNA-dependent RNA polymerase, then use minus strands for templates
Ss –RNA: copied to make mRNA with own polymerase
--- Slide 10 ---
Retroviruses
Retroviruses carry with them the enzyme reverse transcriptase, which makes DNA using RNA as a template
The DNA made copying the viral RNA is inserted into the host DNA
The enzyme is an effective antiviral target (many anti-HIV medications target it)
(credit: modification of work by NIAID, NIH)

Viral infections
Acute infections
Persistent viral infections
Latent: Virus remains in asymptomatic host cell for long periods.
Cold sores, chickenpox/shingles
Chronic: Disease processes occurs over a long period; generally is fatal.
Subacute sclerosing panencephalitis (measles virus)
HIV/AIDS
--- Slide 12 ---
Latent infections
Varicella zoster virus causes chickenpox
Its double-stranded DNA genome becomes incorporated in the host DNA.
After a period of latency, the virus can reactivate in the form of shingles, usually manifesting as a painful, localized rash on one side of the body.
(credit a: modification of work by Erskine Palmer and B.G. Partin—scale-bar data from Matt Russell; credit b: modification of work by Rosmarie Voegtli)

--- Slide 17 ---
Viroids & virusoids
Small infectious RNA molecules that can self-replicate (viroids) & non–self-replicating ssRNAs (virusoids)
Mainly plant infections
Virusoids need a “helper virus” to reproduce
These potatoes have been infected by the potato spindle tuber viroid (PSTV), which is typically spread when infected knives are used to cut healthy potatoes, which are then planted. (credit: Pamela Roberts, University of Florida Institute of Food and Agricultural Sciences, USDA ARS)
--- Slide 18 ---
Prions
Infectious proteins
PrPC: Normal cellular prion protein, on cell surface
PrPSc: Misfolded  protein; accumulates in brain cells, forming plaques
Inherited and transmissible by ingestion, transplant, and surgical instruments
Spongiform encephalopathies:
Animals: Sheep scrapie, mad cow disease
Humans: Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease
