### ⚠️ PARTIAL CREDIT

#### Q3 — pH fill-in-the-blank (0.8 / 1)
**Question:**
If the pH of a solution increases from 4 to 7, the concentration of H⁺ will be ___ times ___ and the concentration of OH⁻ will be ___ times ___ than at pH 4. The solution will be more ___ than before.

**Correct answers:** `1/1000` · `lower` · `1000` · `higher` · `basic`

**What I got wrong:**
For the second blank (after "H⁺ will be 1/1000 times ___"), I likely put **1000** instead of **lower**.
The word bank had both numbers AND direction words — both need to be used correctly.

**Lesson:**
- pH increases → H⁺ **decreases**, OH⁻ **increases**
- pH scale is logarithmic: every 1 unit = 10× change
- pH 4 → pH 7 = 3 units = 10³ = **1000×** change
- Always check: does this blank need a **number** (1000, 1/1000) or a **direction word** (higher, lower)?

### ❌ WRONG

#### Q10 — Bond identification from figure (0 / 1)
**Question:**
Figure shows two water molecules. Bond labeled "B" (solid line, O–H within same molecule). Bond labeled "A" (dashed lines between molecules). What type of bond is "B"?

**My answer:** Hydrogen ❌
**Correct answer:** Polar covalent ✅

**What I got wrong:**
I confused Bond A and Bond B.
- **Bond A** = dashed lines between two water molecules = **hydrogen bond**
- **Bond B** = solid line between O and H within one molecule = **polar covalent**

**Lesson:**
- **Solid line** = covalent bond (atoms sharing electrons within the SAME molecule)
- **Dashed line** = hydrogen bond (weak attraction BETWEEN molecules)
- In any bond diagram: identify whether the bond is WITHIN a molecule or BETWEEN molecules first, then name it
